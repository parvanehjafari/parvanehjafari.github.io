## SECTION 2: PHONE ETIQUETTE & CALL HANDLING (Questions 11–20)

### 11. How would you answer a phone call professionally? (Wie würden Sie einen Anruf professionell beantworten?)

**Answer:** "I would greet the caller with a friendly tone and say something like: 'Good morning, [Company Name], this is [Name], how can I help you?' I make sure to speak clearly, listen carefully, and take notes during the call. At the end, I summarize what was discussed and confirm the next steps before saying goodbye politely."

### 12. What would you do if a caller doesn't speak German well? (Was würden Sie tun, wenn ein Anrufer kein Deutsch spricht?)

**Answer:** "I would speak slowly and clearly, use simple language, and be patient. If English is available, I'd offer to switch. If not, I'd do my best to understand the issue, use written follow-up if possible, or ask a bilingual colleague for support. The key is to remain patient and make sure the customer feels understood."

### 13. How do you handle multiple calls on hold? (Wie gehen Sie mit mehreren Anrufen in der Warteschleife um?)

**Answer:** "I prioritize calls based on urgency and waiting time. I make sure to acknowledge callers quickly, even if I need a moment to look something up. I'd say, 'Thank you for holding, I'm looking into that for you right now.' I stay organized and avoid making a caller wait longer than necessary without an update."

### 14. What would you do if you accidentally gave a customer wrong information? (Was würden Sie tun, wenn Sie einem Kunden versehentlich falsche Informationen geben?)

**Answer:** "I would call the customer back immediately, apologize honestly for the mistake, and provide the correct information. Transparency and honesty are essential in building trust. I would also make a note of the error to prevent it from happening again."

### 15. How do you ensure a professional phone tone? (Wie stellen Sie einen professionellen Telefonton sicher?)

**Answer:** "I smile when I speak — it naturally makes my voice sound friendlier. I sit up straight, speak clearly at a moderate pace, and use the customer's name during the call. I avoid eating or drinking during calls and make sure my background is quiet. I also use polite phrases like 'please,' 'thank you,' and 'I'm happy to help.'"

### 16. How would you handle a silent caller or a prank call? (Wie würden Sie mit einem stummen Anruf oder einem Scherzanruf umgehen?)

**Answer:** "For a silent caller, I'd wait a moment and then politely ask, 'Hello, are you still there? How can I help you?' If there's no response, I'd end the call professionally. For prank calls, I'd remain calm and professional, and if it continues, I'd inform my supervisor. I never react with frustration or rudeness."

### 17. What's the longest you've kept a customer on hold? How did you manage it? (Wie lange haben Sie einen Kunden最长warten lassen?)

**Answer:** "In a previous role, I once had to keep a customer on hold for about five minutes while I resolved a billing issue. I checked in with them every minute to let them know I was still working on it. The customer appreciated the communication and thanked me for resolving the issue thoroughly."

### 18. How do you handle a situation where a customer asks to speak to your manager? (Wie gehen Sie damit um, wenn ein Kunde mit dem Manager sprechen möchte?)

**Answer:** "I first try to resolve the issue myself, saying something like, 'I understand your concern — let me see what I can do for you.' If the customer still insists, I transfer the call politely and provide the manager with a brief summary of the situation so the customer doesn't have to repeat everything."

### 19. Describe your ideal way to end a phone call with a customer. (Beschreiben Sie Ihre ideale Art, einen Telefonat mit einem Kunden zu beenden.)

**Answer:** "Before ending, I always summarize the key points: 'Just to confirm, we've arranged [solution] and you can expect [follow-up] by [date].' Then I ask if there's anything else I can help with. I end with a friendly closing: 'Thank you for calling, [Customer Name]. Have a great day!'"

### 20. How do you handle language barriers during a customer call? (Wie gehen Sie mit Sprachbarrieren bei einem Kundenanruf um?)

**Answer:** "I speak slowly, use simple and clear words, and avoid jargon. I ask confirming questions like 'Did I understand you correctly?' I can also offer to send written instructions via email after the call. If needed, I'd ask a colleague who speaks the customer's language for support."

## SECTION 3: COMPLAINT HANDLING & DIFFICULT CUSTOMERS (Questions 21–32)

### 21. How do you handle difficult or angry customers? (Wie gehen Sie mit schwierigen oder wütenden Kunden um?)

**Answer:** "I stay calm and let the customer express their frustration without interrupting. I acknowledge their feelings by saying, 'I completely understand your frustration.' Then I focus on finding a solution rather than arguing. I ask clarifying questions, explain what I can do, and follow through on my promises."

### 22. Tell me about a time you turned a negative situation into a positive one. (Erzählen Sie von einer Situation, die Sie positiv gewendet haben.)

**Answer:** "A customer called very upset because their order was delayed. I apologized sincerely, tracked the shipment in real time, and arranged express delivery at no extra cost. I called them back with an update and followed up after delivery. The customer later left a positive review and thanked me for the excellent communication."

### 23. How do you handle customer complaints? (Wie gehen Sie mit Kundenbeschwerden um?)

**Answer:** "I follow a clear process: First, I listen carefully to understand the full issue. Then I empathize — 'I understand this must be frustrating.' Next, I clarify what the customer wants and explain the steps I can take. Finally, I follow up to make sure the issue is resolved and the customer is satisfied."

### 24. What would you do if a customer is verbally abusive? (Was würden Sie tun, wenn ein Kunde verbal aggressiv wird?)

**Answer:** "I would remain calm and professional. I'd say, 'I want to help you, but I need us to have a respectful conversation.' If the abuse continues, I would follow company protocol, which might involve ending the call or escalating to a supervisor. I never take verbal abuse personally."

### 25. A customer threatens to leave a negative review. How do you respond? (Ein Kunde droht mit einer negativen Bewertung. Wie reagieren Sie?)

**Answer:** "I take their concern seriously and say, 'I understand your disappointment and I want to make this right.' I focus on solving their problem rather than the threat itself. Most customers just want to be heard and helped. If I resolve their issue well, the negative review often becomes a positive one."

### 26. How do you handle a situation where you cannot fulfill a customer's request? (Wie gehen Sie damit um, wenn Sie einen Kundenwunsch nicht erfüllen können?)

**Answer:** "I explain the reason honestly and respectfully — 'Unfortunately, I'm unable to do that because [policy/reason], but here's what I can do instead.' I always offer an alternative solution or next step so the customer doesn't feel like they've hit a dead end."

### 27. How would you handle a complaint about a product or service you're not familiar with? (Wie würden Sie eine Beschwerde über ein Produkt oder einen Service bearbeiten, den Sie nicht kennen?)

**Answer:** "I would be honest and say, 'Let me look into this for you to make sure I give you the right information.' I'd check the knowledge base, ask a colleague, or escalate if needed. I never guess — accuracy is more important than speed."

### 28. What does empathy mean to you in a customer service role? (Was bedeutet Empathie in einer Kundenservice-Rolle für Sie?)

**Answer:** "Empathy means putting yourself in the customer's shoes and genuinely understanding how they feel. It doesn't mean agreeing with everything they say, but showing them that you care about their experience. When customers feel heard, conversations become easier and solutions come faster."

### 29. How do you manage stress after a difficult call? (Wie gehen Sie mit Stress nach einem schwierigen Gespräch um?)

**Answer:** "I take a few deep breaths, stretch briefly, and reset mentally before the next call. I remind myself that the customer's anger wasn't personal — they were frustrated with the situation. I stay focused and maintain a positive attitude for the next caller."

### 30. Describe a time when you received unfair criticism from a customer. How did you handle it? (Beschreiben Sie eine Situation, in der Sie unfair kritisiert wurden.)

**Answer:** "A customer once blamed me for a shipping delay that was actually caused by the carrier. Instead of getting defensive, I apologized for the inconvenience and focused on finding a solution. I tracked the package, updated the customer regularly, and they eventually thanked me for my patience and effort."

### 31. How would you handle a complaint from a customer who keeps calling about the same issue? (Wie würden Sie mit einem Kunden umgehen, der immer wieder wegen desselben Problems anruft?)

**Answer:** "I'd review the customer's history to understand the full context, acknowledge that the issue hasn't been resolved to their satisfaction, and escalate to a specialist if needed. I'd also document the case thoroughly so the next person can pick up without the customer having to repeat themselves."

### 32. What steps would you take if a customer is dissatisfied with your solution? (Welche Schritte würden Sie unternehmen, wenn ein Kunde mit Ihrer Lösung unzufrieden ist?)

**Answer:** "I'd listen to their specific concern about my proposed solution, ask clarifying questions, and explore alternative options. If I've exhausted my options, I'd escalate to a supervisor or specialist, always keeping the customer informed about what's happening."

## SECTION 4: COMMUNICATION SKILLS (Questions 33–40)

### 33. How do you explain complex information to a customer in simple terms? (Wie erklären Sie komplexe Informationen einfach?)

**Answer:** "I avoid jargon and technical terms. I use analogies or step-by-step instructions. For example, instead of saying 'reset your DNS settings,' I'd say 'go to your phone settings, find the network section, and tap on reset.' I check understanding by asking, 'Does that make sense?' or 'Would you like me to repeat that?'"

### 34. How do you adapt your communication style to different customers? (Wie passen Sie Ihren Kommunikationsstil an verschiedene Kunden an?)

**Answer:** "I observe the customer's tone and pace. If someone is formal, I stay professional. If they're casual, I'm friendly but still respectful. For older customers, I speak clearly and slowly. For younger customers, I'm more direct. The key is to make each customer feel comfortable."

### 35. How do you practice active listening? (Wie üben Sie aktives Zuhören?)

**Answer:** "I focus fully on the caller, avoid distractions, and don't interrupt. I paraphrase what the customer said — 'So if I understand correctly, you're saying…' — to confirm understanding. I also take notes on key points so I can address everything before the call ends."

### 36. How would you handle a situation where you and a customer disagree? (Wie würden Sie eine Situation handhaben, in der Sie und ein Kunde unterschiedlicher Meinung sind?)

**Answer:** "I stay respectful and don't argue. I focus on facts and policies, saying something like, 'I understand your point. Here's how our policy works, and here's what I can do for you.' The goal is to find a solution, not to win an argument."

### 37. What do you think makes a great communicator in customer service? (Was macht einengreaten Kommunikator im Kundenservice aus?)

**Answer:** "Clarity, patience, and empathy. A great communicator speaks clearly without jargon, listens without interrupting, and adapts their tone to the situation. They also follow up and confirm that the customer understood the information."

### 38. How do you handle a situation where you need to say "no" to a customer? (Wie gehen Sie damit um, wenn Sie einem Kunden "nein" sagen müssen?)

**Answer:** "I explain the reason honestly and respectfully, then immediately offer an alternative. For example: 'I'm sorry, but I can't offer a refund after 30 days. However, I can offer you a discount on your next purchase or help you with a different solution.' The key is to be helpful even when saying no."

### 39. How do you handle cultural differences in communication? (Wie gehen Sie mit kulturellen Unterschieden in der Kommunikation um?)

**Answer:** "I'm respectful of different communication styles and cultural norms. I avoid assumptions, use inclusive language, and stay open-minded. If I'm unsure about something, I ask politely rather than guess. In a multicultural city like Berlin, this skill is essential."

### 40. How would you handle giving feedback to a coworker? (Wie würden Sie einem Kollegen Feedback geben?)

**Answer:** "I'd be direct but kind. I'd choose a private moment, focus on the behavior not the person, and suggest a concrete improvement. For example: 'I noticed the call notes were incomplete — it would help the team if we could add more detail.' I'd frame it as teamwork, not criticism."

## SECTION 11: ROLE-PLAY & PRACTICAL SCENARIOS (Questions 73–77)

### 73. Role-play: A customer calls and says, "I've been waiting 30 minutes for a response to my email!" How do you respond? (Rollenspiel: Ein Kunde ruft an und sagt: „Ich warte seit 30 Minuten auf eine Antwort auf meine E-Mail!")

**Answer:** "I understand your frustration, and I sincerely apologize for the delay. Let me look into your case right now and help you directly over the phone. Can you please tell me your reference number or name so I can pull up your file? I want to make sure we resolve this for you today."

### 74. Role-play: A customer says they want to cancel their subscription. What do you do? (Rollenspiel: Ein Kunde sagt, er möchte sein Abonnement kündigen. Was tun Sie?)

**Answer:** "I understand, and I'm sorry to hear that. Before I process the cancellation, may I ask what's causing you to leave? Sometimes I can offer a solution or a better plan that might work for you. If you still wish to cancel, I'll take care of it right away and confirm the details by email."

### 75. Role-play: A customer is speaking very fast in German and you can't follow. How do you handle it? (Rollenspiel: Ein Kunde spricht sehr schnell auf Deutsch und Sie können nicht folgen.)

**Answer:** "'Entschuldigung, könnten Sie bitte etwas langsamer sprechen? Ich möchte sicherstellen, dass ich alles richtig verstehe, um Ihnen bestmöglich zu helfen.' I never bluff — asking for slower speech is professional and shows I care about getting it right."

### 76. Role-play: A customer insists on a refund but your policy only allows exchanges. What do you say? (Rollenspiel: Ein Kunde besteht auf eine Rückerstattung, aber Ihre Politik erlaubt nur Umtausch.)

**Answer:** "I completely understand your preference for a refund. Unfortunately, our current policy allows exchanges or store credit for this type of product. However, I can offer you a full exchange for any item of equal or greater value, or store credit that never expires. Would either of those work for you?"

### 77. Role-play: It's your first day on the job and a customer asks you a question you don't know the answer to. What do you say? (Rollenspiel: Es ist Ihr erster Tag und ein Kunde stellt eine Frage, deren Antwort Sie nicht kennen.)

**Answer:** "That's a great question. Since I'm just getting started with our systems, let me quickly check with a colleague to make sure I give you the most accurate answer. I'll be right back — thank you for your patience."

## SECTION 12: QUESTIONS TO ASK THE INTERVIEWER (Questions 78–80)

### 78. What does a typical training process look like for new customer service employees? (Wie sieht ein typischer Einarbeitungsprozess für neue Kundenservice-Mitarbeiter aus?)

**Why ask this:** Shows you care about doing the job well from day one. Employers appreciate candidates who take training seriously. In Germany, structured Einarbeitung is common and valued.

### 79. What is the team structure like? How many people work on each shift? (Wie ist die Teamstruktur? Wie viele Leute arbeiten pro Schicht?)

**Why ask this:** This helps you understand the work environment and shows you're thinking about how you'd fit into the team. It's especially relevant for Mini-Job positions where you may only work specific shifts.

### 80. What qualities make someone successful in this role at your company? (Welche Eigenschaften machen jemanden in dieser Rolle bei Ihrem Unternehmen erfolgreich?)

**Why ask this:** This shows ambition and a desire to excel, not just get hired. It also gives you insight into what the employer values most, so you can tailor your follow-up thank-you email.

## BONUS TIPS FOR GERMAN MINI-JOB INTERVIEWS

1. **Arrive 10 minutes early** — punctuality (Pünktlichkeit) is highly valued in Germany.
2. **Dress business casual** — even for call center roles.
3. **Bring printed copies** of your CV, Arbeitszeugnis (if available), and work permit.
4. **Use polite German** — "Sie" form always, "bitte" and "danke" frequently.
5. **Show willingness to learn** — employers value attitude over perfect German.
6. **Ask questions at the end** — about training, team structure, or typical shift patterns.
7. **Mention your Minijob tax status** — show you understand the 520€/month rule.
8. **Reference your Ausländerbehörde status** if applicable — show your work permit is valid.
